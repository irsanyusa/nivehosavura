Meet The 11 Queens Competing on 'RuPaul's Drag Race UK vs The World' Season 2

Publish date: 2024-06-14

TALENTRECAP.COM More Shows RuPaul's Drag Race

Reading Time: 2 minutes

The highly anticipated second season of RuPaul’s Drag Race UK vs The World has finally revealed its cast of 11 fierce queens ready to battle it out for the crown. The new lineup brings together renowned drag artists from various international editions of the hit reality competition series for a showdown on the main stage.

Diverse Group of International Drag Superstars Competing

The cast for RuPaul’s Drag Race UK vs The World Season 2 features an incredibly diverse group of talented queens representing several countries. Fan favorites like Mayhem Miller (USA), La Grande Dame (France), and Scarlet Envy (USA) will compete against newcomers like Marina Summers of Drag Race Philippines and Hannah Conda of Drag Race Down Under.

Other queens joining the competition are Choriza May and Jonbers Blonde from Drag Race UK, Arantxa Castilla La Mancha from Drag Race Espana, Keta Minaj from Drag Race Holland. Additionally, Gothy Kendoll from Drag Race UK, and Tia Kofi from Drag Race UK are set to bring their signature flair to the all new season. Each queen has a unique drag perspective shaped by their home country’s drag culture.

Having such an eclectic mix of international drag stars makes this an unprecedented Drag Race showdown. Queens will need to demonstrate versatility and an ability to adapt as they face off in challenges.

For many competitors, this will be their first time competing under RuPaul‘s guidance. However, some veterans are coming back to prove themselves again on the main stage.

Fan Favorites, Underdogs Bring Drama to The New Season

While some queens like Mayhem Miller and La Grande Dame have competed in their respective Drag Race seasons before, this will be the first time they battle against each other. Adding Drag Race alums into the mix raises the stakes. These seasoned pros will set a high bar, but they also have reputations to uphold.

Meanwhile, relative newcomers like Marina Summers and Hannah Conda might be underestimated by the other competitors. These sneaky underdogs could wind up surprising everyone with breakout performances. Having fan faves compete against rising stars will make for riveting reality TV drama.

There will also surely be personality clashes and tensions between queens of different backgrounds and languages. On the other hand, bringing together so many diverse drag perspectives could lead to innovation and creativity. Queens may push each other artistically in exciting new directions.
