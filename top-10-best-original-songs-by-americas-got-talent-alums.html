Top 10 Best Original Songs by 'America's Got Talent' Alums

Publish date: 2024-07-01

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent America's Got Talent

NBC

Reading Time: 3 minutes

America’s Got Talent has been a platform that has not only showcased incredible talents but also served as a launchpad for many aspiring musicians. Throughout its illustrious history, AGT has produced several talented musicians who have continued to captivate audiences with their original music. In this article, we present a selection of ten remarkable original songs by AGT alum, highlighting why these compositions have become our favorites.

Top 10 Best Original Songs by AGT Alum

10. “Who I Am” by Wyn Starks

Wyn Starks delivered an emotionally charged audition on AGT with his original song “Who I Am.” In a heartfelt tribute to his late twin brother, Starks poured his soul into the soulful number, leaving a lasting impact on both the judges and the audience. His powerful vocals and raw vulnerability resonated deeply, particularly moving judge Sofia Vergara to tears.

Joseph Allen made a significant impact on AGT with his heartfelt original song, “Footprint.” In a competition largely dominated by singers, Allen’s rousing performance as a rapper captivated both the audience and the judges. With his powerful lyrics, he expressed his aspirations of leaving a lasting impact on the world, striking a chord with listeners.

8. “You Can Have Him Jolene” by Chapel Hart

Chapel Hart, a talented country trio, made waves on AGT Season 17 with their rendition of their original song “You Can Have Him Jolene.” Originally released as a single from their self-released album, The Girls Are Back in Town, this track caught the attention of both the judges and the audience.

7. “Sounds Like Something I’d Do” by Drake Milligan

Drake Milligan made a lasting impression on AGT Season 17 with his audition performance of his original song “Sounds Like Something I’d Do.” Armed with his guitar and supported by his band, Milligan then showcased his musical talent and songwriting skills.

6. “Daydream” by Lily Meola

Lily Meola‘s emotional and poignant original song, “Daydream,” propelled her to the coveted Golden Buzzer moment on AGT. Written during a challenging period in her life, as she navigated the complexities of her first label deal and her mother’s cancer diagnosis, Meola’s resilience and talent shone through in this heartfelt composition.

5. “What Makes You Sad” by Sam Cieri

During AGT Season 17, Sam Cieri, the lead singer of Nicotine Dolls, delivered a fiery performance of his original song “What Makes You Sad.” Combining elements of blues and rock, Cieri’s soulful and raspy vocals added a unique texture to the band’s catchy hook.

4. “Pretty Little Thing” by Courtney Hadwin

Courtney Hadwin, known for her powerhouse vocals and dynamic stage presence, mesmerized the audience with her original song “Pretty Little Thing” during AGT: The Champions. Her electrifying performance earned her standing ovations from the judges and also showcased her unique style.

3. “Try” by Mandy Harvey

Mandy Harvey, a finalist in AGT Season 12, wowed audiences with her incredible vocal talent. “Try” showcases her impressive songwriting skills, combining heartfelt lyrics and also a beautiful melody to create an enchanting and empowering experience.

2. “I Don’t Know My Name” by Grace VanderWaal

Grace VanderWaal, the winner of AGT Season 11, captured hearts with her poignant songwriting and soulful voice. “I Don’t Know My Name” remains an enduring favorite for its introspective and heartfelt lyrics and melody, inviting listeners to delve into the depths of self-discovery.

1. “It’s Okay” by Nightbirde

Nightbirde, the stage name of AGT Season 16 contestant Jane Marczewski, touched the hearts of millions with her original song “It’s Okay.” This soul-stirring composition, written during her battle with cancer, radiates resilience and hope.
