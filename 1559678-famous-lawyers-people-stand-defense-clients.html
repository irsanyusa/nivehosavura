20 famous lawyers: The best people who stand in defence of their clients

Publish date: 2024-08-05

Lawyers play a vital role in society, often described as the guardians of justice. Unlike some celebrities who gain their fame for the wrong reasons, lawyers have to champion the cause of justice and make a lasting impact on the lives of individuals and the broader community. Amidst all challenges, famous lawyers have shaped legal precedents, driven social change, and inspired others to pursue careers in law.

Law and fame often intersect between high-profile legal cases and iconic legal practitioners. Certain legal cases become widely recognised due to their social, political, or cultural significance, thrusting the lawyers involved into the spotlight. Famous lawyers can attain celebrity status through their legal acumen and representation of notable clients.

1. Robert Shapiro

Robert Shapiro tops the list of the best lawyers of all time. He is an American lawyer born on 2 September 1942 in Plainfield, New Jersey, USA. Shapiro is best known for his legal career and high-profile cases. Shapiro gained national and international prominence as the lead defence attorneyfor O.J. Simpson during the 1995 murder trial.

The O.J. Simpson case was notable for its racial dynamics, celebrity involvement, and extensive media coverage, making him the most famous lawyer. He is also one of the founders of the Glaser Weil Fink Jacobs Howard Avchen & Shapiro, LLP law farm.

2. Franklin Roosevelt

Franklin Roosevelt, a lawyer by profession, was the 32nd president of the United States from 1933 until his passing in 1945. Notably, he is the sole President in US history to secure victory in four presidential elections.

Before entering politics, he was admitted to the New York Bar and engaged in legal practice for a brief period. He commenced his political career by winning his initial New York State Senate election.

3. Thurgood Marshall

Thurgood Marshall was a prominent figure in American law, renowned for his career as a lawyer and the 96th Associate Justice of the United States Supreme Court.

His most significant achievement lies in his role as the lead counsel in the landmark case of Brown v. Board of Education. This was a pivotal moment in the fight against racial segregation in schools, reshaping the course of American history.

4. Marcia Clark

Marcia Clark is an American attorney, author, and television personality. Born on 31 August 1953 in Berkeley, California, Marcia Clark began her legal career as a public defender and later worked as a prosecutor. She gained widespread recognition as the lead prosecutor in the infamous O.J. Simpson murder trial in 1995.

5. William Howard Taft

William Howard Taft, one of America's most successful lawyers, served as the 10th Chief Justice of the Supreme Court and the 27th President of the United States. He was the first person to hold the country's highest executive and judicial positions.

His legal expertise and commitment to justice were evident throughout his career, making him one of the most esteemed legal figures in American history.

6. Robert Kardashian

Robert Kardashian is the father of the prominent and influential American family in the reality television, The Kardashians. Besides being their father, he was one of the most famous lawyers in American history. He gained public recognition due to his involvement in a high-profile case, the O.J. Simpson murder trial.

7. Andrew Jackson

Andrew Jackson, widely recognised as the seventh president of the United States, achieved notable distinctions as a lawyer before his political career took off. His legal career was marked by notable cases, including his representation of Rachel Robard in a high-profile divorce case.

12. Claudia Gordon

Claudia Gordon is a prominent figure in the field of disability policy and a former advisor on disability issues to the President Barack Obama administration. Claudia is recognised not only as a highly regarded lawyer within the United States but also as a trailblazing deaf African-American woman.

14. Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi is widely recognised as an activist and a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence. After studying law in London, he returned to India and practised law in South Africa, where he also became involved in civil rights and social justice issues.

15. Gloria Allred

Gloria Allred, a prominent civil rights lawyer in the United States, has been involved in several significant legal battles, representing well-known clients.
